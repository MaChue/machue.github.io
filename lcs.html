Unit 1

In unit 1, we have learned:
  • Responsibilities and code of conduct of a computer professional.
  • Code of Ethics of a computer professional according to IEEE (Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers) and ACM (Association for Computing Machinery) organisation standard.
  • Different data protection rules according to different regions, such as GDPR (Europe’s General Data Protection Regulations), CCPA (California Consumer Privacy Act), LGPD (Brazil’s Lei Geral de Proteção de Dados), POPI (South Africa’s Protection of personal information).
  • Different kinds of software security risks, threats and challenges such as DoS, DDoS, Viruses, Worms, Trojan Horse, Spyware, Spam.
  • Protection methods and cures such as Firewalls, Proxy Servers, Antivirus Software, Cryptography.
  • Different Cryptography methods for data protection such as Symmetric-key encryption, public-key encryption, Certificate authorities (CAs)

Activities

  • We created our first e-portfolio that reflects our professional experiences and learning activities.
  • We discussed the challenges of our desired Computer Science role and how we will achieve it.
  • In our first live seminar, we introduced each other and shared our professional backgrounds, discussed Unit 1 activities with the tutor and listened to the tutor’s lecture.

Learning Outcomes

  • After finishing Unit 1, I got more understanding of the best professional practice, code of conduct, responsibilities, code of ethics and challenges of a computer professional, and more awareness of different data production rules of different countries, several kinds of software security risks, protection and cures of the risks.
  • By posting discussions on the discussion forum, I can engage different aspects, points of view and feedback of my fellow classmates.
  • In the live seminar section, I can learn about different experiences and points of view of my tutor and classmates.

Challenges and Difficulties

  • The main difficulty I am currently facing and will be facing through the module is the times of live seminars. The live seminars are always held at 6 PM GMT in the UK. Because of the timezone difference, It becomes 2 AM GST in Singapore. That’s why I have a hard time concentrating on lectures and to actively involve in discussion during one hour of lecture time. The understanding of the lecture is ok because I can relisten the recorded videos.
