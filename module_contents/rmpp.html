Unit 1

In unit 1, we have learned:
  • Introduction to Research Purposes
  • Application of the Scientific Method
  • Distinguishing Between Reasoning Types
  • Ethical Research Practices

Activities

  • Introduction to Research Purposes: We learned that research serves three main purposes: to explore, describe, and explain. These purposes help us to familiarise ourselves with a topic, describe our findings, and answer the question "why?".
  • Scientific Method: We delved into the history and application of the scientific method, which involves observation, hypothesis, reasoning, and testing. This method is a cornerstone of problem-solving in research.
  • Deductive and Inductive Reasoning: We examined the differences between deductive and inductive reasoning. Deductive reasoning moves from an idea to an observation, while inductive reasoning moves from an observation to an idea.
  • Research Ethics: We explored the key principles of research ethics, including respect, beneficence, justice, and respect for law and public interest. These principles ensure that research is conducted ethically, protecting the rights and wellbeing of participants.

Learning Outcomes

  • Understanding the Purpose of Research: We comprehended the various reasons for conducting research and the significance of each purpose in both academic and professional settings.
  • Application of the Scientific Method: We learned how to apply the scientific method in our research, including the roles of observation, hypothesis, reasoning, and testing.
  • Distinguishing Between Reasoning Types: We differentiated between deductive and inductive reasoning and understood their applications in research.
  • Ethical Research Practices: We gained knowledge about the ethical considerations necessary for conducting research, particularly when it involves human participants. We learned to apply the principles of respect, beneficence, justice, and respect for law and public interest in our research projects.

Challenges and Difficulties

  • Understanding Complex Concepts: Grasping the nuances of deductive and inductive reasoning was initially challenging. It required careful study and application to different scenarios to fully understand these concepts.
  • Ethical Considerations: Navigating the ethical implications of research was complex. Ensuring that all ethical principles were adhered to while designing research required careful consideration and planning.
  • Balancing Theory and Practice: Integrating theoretical knowledge with practical applications of the scientific method and ethical research practices was challenging but ultimately rewarding.

Unit 2

In unit 2, we have learned:
  • Formulating and Revising Research Questions
  • Components of a Research Proposal
  • Conducting and Presenting a Literature Review

Activities

  • Research Questions: We learned to formulate and revise research questions, starting from an initial research idea and refining it through pertinent questions to prepare a solid research proposal.
  • Research Proposal: We explored the different components that make up a research proposal and how to present our thoughts effectively.
  • Literature Review: We were introduced to the process of completing a literature review, including how to conduct a literature search, critique the literature, and present a review.

Learning Outcomes

  • Examining Suitable Research Topics: We examined the characteristics that make up a suitable research topic.
  • Formulating Research Ideas: We explored rational and creative methods for formulating a research idea.
  • Crafting Research Questions and Proposals: We identified means of transforming research ideas into well-crafted research questions and proposals.
  • Conducting and Presenting Literature Reviews: We learned to conduct a literature search, critique the literature, and present a comprehensive literature review.

Challenges and Difficulties

  • Defining Research Topics: Narrowing down and defining the topic of research was challenging and required multiple revisions to ensure clarity and focus.
  • Conducting Literature Reviews: Finding relevant and credible sources for the literature review was time-consuming and required meticulous effort to ensure comprehensive coverage of the topic.
  • Presenting Research Proposals: Structuring and presenting the research proposal in a clear and concise manner was challenging but essential for effectively communicating research ideas.

Unit 3

In unit 3, we have learned:
  • Research Design Concepts
  • Exploratory and Conclusive Research
  • Quantitative, Qualitative, and Mixed Methods Research
  • Primary and Secondary Data Collection Methods

Activities

  • Research Design: We learned about research design, which involves planning what to do to answer a research question. We covered exploratory and conclusive research designs, understanding their applications and differences.
  • Research Methods: We explored three main research methods—qualitative, quantitative, and mixed methods research. We learned how these methods determine the type of data collected and analysed to answer research questions.
  • Data Collection: We studied primary and secondary data collection methods, including surveys, interviews, experiments, and observations, understanding their relevance to different research methods.

Learning Outcomes

  • Understanding Research Methods: We gained a comprehensive understanding of various research methods and their applications in different research scenarios.
  • Data Collection Methods: We learned about the different data collection methods associated with qualitative, quantitative, and mixed methods research.
  • Application to Research Areas: We developed insights into which research methods and data collection techniques are best suited to our specific areas of research.

Challenges and Difficulties

  • Choosing Research Methods: Selecting the most appropriate research method for a given research question was challenging and required careful consideration of the research objectives.
  • Data Collection Techniques: Understanding the best data collection techniques for different research methods and ensuring their proper implementation was complex but crucial for the accuracy of research findings.
  • Balancing Methodologies: Integrating qualitative and quantitative methods in mixed methods research posed a challenge, requiring a clear strategy to balance and interpret data from both approaches effectively.

Unit 4

In unit 4, we have learned:
  • Understanding Case Studies
  • Utilising Focus Groups for Data Collection
  • Qualitative and Quantitative Observations

Activities

  • Case Studies: We explored the use of case studies for in-depth research on individuals or groups. While case studies can generate hypotheses and provide a deeper understanding of phenomena, they are limited in predicting cause and effect due to potential researcher bias and the presence of atypical respondents.
  • Focus Groups: We learned about focus groups, a qualitative research method where a small group of people (6-10) discuss their insights on a particular topic. Proper selection of participants is crucial to ensure they have common backgrounds and experiences, representing the target market effectively.
  • Observation Methods: We studied qualitative and quantitative observation methods. Qualitative observation involves monitoring characteristics from a distance to ensure natural behaviour, while quantitative observation focuses on collecting numerical data for statistical analysis. Both methods are essential for comprehensive data collection.

Learning Outcomes

  • Carrying Out Data Collection Methods: We understood how to effectively carry out case studies, focus groups, and observational methods.
  • Choosing Suitable Methods: We learned to identify which data collection method would be suitable for specific investigations based on the research question and objectives.
  • Types of Data: We considered the types of data obtained from each method and how they contribute to answering research questions.

Challenges and Difficulties

  • Researcher Bias in Case Studies: Ensuring objectivity in case studies was challenging due to potential bias, which can affect the reliability and validity of findings.
  • Selecting Focus Group Participants: Choosing the right participants for focus groups to ensure they are representative of the target market and have comparable experiences was complex.
  • Balancing Observational Methods: Integrating qualitative and quantitative observations required a clear strategy to ensure comprehensive and accurate data collection.

Unit 5

In unit 5, we have learned:
  • Conducting In-depth Interviews
  • Designing and Implementing Surveys
  • Understanding Population and Sample Definitions
  • Pre- and Post-testing Methods

Activities

  • Interviews: We explored the use of in-depth interviews as a qualitative research method. These one-on-one conversations allow researchers to gather detailed information from respondents, providing deep insights into specific topics.
  • Surveys: We studied the design and implementation of surveys, a key quantitative research method. Surveys can explore opinions, trends, and other quantitative data, often through online platforms for greater accessibility.
  • Population and Sample: We discussed the definitions of population and sample, understanding how to select representative samples from a population to ensure the validity and reliability of research findings.
  • Pre- and Post-testing: We introduced the concept of pre- and post-testing, which provides a "before-and-after" perspective on the impact of new processes or systems.

Learning Outcomes

  • Understanding Research Methods: We understood how interviews and surveys fit into research, and when each method might be useful for investigations.
  • Implementing Pre- and Post-testing: We learned how pre- and post-testing can be used in research to measure the effectiveness of interventions.

Challenges and Difficulties

  • Designing Effective Surveys: Creating surveys that accurately capture the required data without introducing bias was challenging.
  • Conducting In-depth Interviews: Ensuring that interviews remain focused and productive while allowing respondents to provide detailed insights required careful planning and execution.
  • Balancing Pre- and Post-testing: Integrating pre- and post-testing methods effectively to measure changes and impacts posed a challenge, requiring precise planning and analysis.

Unit 6

In unit 6, we have learned:
  • Differences Between Questionnaires and Surveys
  • Designing Effective Questionnaires
  • Methods of Obtaining Responses
  • Analysing Questionnaire Data

Activities

  • Understanding Questionnaires and Surveys: We clarified that while a survey is a comprehensive method for gathering and analysing data, a questionnaire is the set of questions used to conduct a survey.
  • Designing Questionnaires: We learned how to design effective questionnaires to gather the necessary information from respondents. This includes deciding between open or closed questions based on the type of research (quantitative, qualitative, or mixed methods).
  • Improving Responses: We discussed various methods to improve the quality and quantity of responses obtained from questionnaires, ensuring that the data collected is reliable and valid.
  • Data Analysis: We considered how to analyse responses from different types of questions to derive meaningful insights that answer the research question.

Learning Outcomes

  • Identifying Quality Questionnaires: We learned to distinguish between well-designed and poorly designed questionnaires, understanding the elements that contribute to the effectiveness of a questionnaire.
  • Designing Questionnaires: We developed the skills to design questionnaires tailored to specific research needs, ensuring they are comprehensive and effective in gathering required data.
  • Data Analysis Techniques: We gained insights into the methods for analysing data obtained from questionnaires, understanding how to process both qualitative and quantitative responses.

Challenges and Difficulties

  • Questionnaire Design: Creating questions that are clear, unbiased, and capable of eliciting useful responses was challenging. Ensuring the questionnaire aligns with the research objectives required careful planning.
  • Improving Response Rates: Encouraging respondents to complete questionnaires honestly and thoroughly was difficult, especially when dealing with online surveys.
  • Data Analysis: Analysing data from mixed methods questionnaires (containing both open and closed questions) posed a challenge, requiring the use of various analytical techniques to draw comprehensive conclusions.

Unit 7

In unit 7, we have learned:
  • Understanding Validity, Generalisability, and Reliability
  • Differences Between Qualitative and Quantitative Data
  • Data Cleansing and Validation Processes
  • Analyzing and Presenting Research Results

Activities

  • Validity, Generalisability, and Reliability: We explored how these concepts impact research design and the conclusions drawn from data. Validity ensures the accuracy of findings, generalisability determines if results apply to broader contexts, and reliability measures the consistency of results over time.
  • Qualitative vs. Quantitative Data: We discussed the differences between qualitative data (non-numerical insights) and quantitative data (numerical insights), and how each type requires different analysis methods.
  • Data Cleansing and Validation: We learned about the importance of data cleansing (removing inaccuracies) and validation (ensuring data quality) before conducting any analysis.
  • Analyzing and Presenting Results: We covered techniques for analyzing data and effectively presenting results to support research questions and hypotheses.

Learning Outcomes

  • Impact on Research Design: We understood how validity, generalisability, and reliability affect research design and the interpretation of results.
  • Data Analysis Techniques: We learned various techniques for analyzing both qualitative and quantitative data.
  • Effective Presentation of Results: We developed skills to present research findings in a clear and concise manner, ensuring they support the research objectives.

Challenges and Difficulties

  • Ensuring Validity: Maintaining the accuracy of findings while minimizing biases was challenging, requiring careful planning and implementation.
  • Balancing Data Types: Integrating and analyzing qualitative and quantitative data posed a challenge, requiring a comprehensive approach to ensure accurate interpretation.
  • Data Cleansing and Validation: Ensuring the quality of data through cleansing and validation processes was time-consuming but essential for reliable results.
  • Presenting Complex Results: Effectively presenting complex research findings in an understandable way required thoughtful organization and clear communication.

Unit 8

In unit 8, we have learned:
  • Understanding Levels of Quantitative Data
  • Describing Data Using Measures of Location and Spread
  • Concept of Statistical Inference and Hypothesis Testing
  • Analyzing and Interpreting Data to Draw Conclusions

Activities

  • Levels of Quantitative Data: We explored different levels of measurement, such as nominal, ordinal, interval, and ratio scales, and their implications for data analysis.
  • Measures of Location and Spread: We learned to summarize data using measures of location (mean, median, mode) and spread (range, variance, standard deviation) to provide a comprehensive view of data distribution.
  • Statistical Inference: We discussed the concept of inference, making predictions or generalizations about a population based on sample data, and the importance of probability in quantifying uncertainty.
  • Hypothesis Testing: We covered hypothesis testing methods to determine the validity of assumptions about population parameters, using statistical evidence to accept or reject hypotheses.

Learning Outcomes

  • Identifying Levels of Measurement: We learned to identify and differentiate between various levels of quantitative data, crucial for selecting appropriate analytical methods.
  • Producing Summary Measures: We developed skills to calculate and interpret measures of location and spread, providing insights into data central tendency and variability.
  • Performing Hypothesis Tests: We gained the ability to conduct hypothesis tests, including formulating null and alternative hypotheses, selecting test statistics, and making informed decisions based on test results.

Challenges and Difficulties

  • Understanding Statistical Concepts: Grasping the foundational concepts of statistical inference and hypothesis testing required dedicated effort and practice.
  • Data Summarization: Summarizing data accurately using measures of location and spread was challenging, particularly when dealing with large datasets.
  • Conducting Hypothesis Tests: Formulating appropriate hypotheses and selecting the correct test statistics for different scenarios required careful consideration and understanding of statistical principles.
  • Interpreting Results: Interpreting the results of hypothesis tests and drawing meaningful conclusions from data involved critical thinking and analytical skills.

Unit 9

In unit 9, we have learned:
  • Understanding Methods for Analysing Qualitative Data
  • Interpretation and Presentation of Qualitative Data
  • Using Software Tools for Efficient Data Coding and Analysis
  • Presenting Qualitative Data Through Charts and Graphs

Activities

  • Methods of Qualitative Data Analysis: We explored various methods for analysing qualitative data, such as thematic analysis, content analysis, and grounded theory, to identify patterns and themes in textual data.
  • Data Interpretation: We learned to interpret qualitative data while being aware of potential biases and maintaining objectivity, despite the subjective nature of such data.
  • Use of Software Tools: We discussed the use of software tools like NVivo and ATLAS.ti for coding and analysing qualitative data efficiently, ensuring accurate and reliable results.
  • Data Presentation: We examined how to present qualitative data using charts and graphs, such as word clouds, thematic maps, and frequency tables, to visually communicate findings.

Learning Outcomes

  • Understanding Analysis Methods: We developed an understanding of various qualitative data analysis methods and their applications in research.
  • Effective Data Interpretation: We gained skills in interpreting qualitative data accurately and presenting findings in a coherent and meaningful manner.
  • Efficient Data Coding: We learned to use software tools for data coding and analysis, enhancing the efficiency and reliability of our research.
  • Visual Data Presentation: We acquired the ability to present qualitative data effectively using appropriate visual aids, improving the communication of our research findings.

Challenges and Difficulties

  • Subjectivity in Data Interpretation: Maintaining objectivity while interpreting qualitative data posed challenges due to the inherent subjectivity of such data.
  • Complexity of Coding: Coding qualitative data accurately and efficiently required careful attention to detail and familiarity with software tools.
  • Effective Data Presentation: Finding the best ways to visually present qualitative data in a clear and engaging manner was challenging, especially when dealing with complex themes and patterns.
  • Managing Bias: Ensuring that personal biases did not influence the analysis and interpretation of qualitative data was a critical challenge.

Unit 10

In unit 10, we have learned:
  • Understanding the Importance of Research Writing
  • Structuring a Dissertation
  • Preparing for Research Writing
  • Finalising a Project Proposal

Activities

  • Research Reporting: We discussed the fundamentals of research writing, including its significance in communicating technical knowledge effectively in tech-related businesses.
  • Dissertation Structure: We explored the various sections of a dissertation, such as the introduction, literature review, methodology, results, discussion, and conclusion, and how to approach each section.
  • Preparation for Writing: We focused on how to prepare ourselves for writing a comprehensive dissertation, including time management, planning, and drafting techniques.
  • Project Proposal: We reviewed our work on selecting a research area, conducting a literature review, and considering suitable research methods, culminating in the preparation of a project proposal.

Learning Outcomes

  • Research Writing Skills: We developed skills in research writing, essential for preparing dissertations, research proposals, and papers for publication in peer-reviewed journals.
  • Dissertation Structuring: We learned how to effectively structure a dissertation and understand the key components that need to be included in each section.
  • Project Proposal Preparation: We acquired the ability to finalise a project proposal, integrating all aspects of our research, including literature review, methodology, and ethical considerations.
  • Time Management: We enhanced our time management skills, crucial for the successful completion of extensive writing tasks such as dissertations.

Challenges and Difficulties

  • Adapting to Extensive Writing: Adjusting to the demands of extensive writing tasks was challenging, especially for those not accustomed to writing large documents.
  • Ensuring Clarity and Cohesion: Maintaining clarity and cohesion throughout the dissertation, ensuring that each section flows logically, posed significant challenges.
  • Balancing Research and Writing: Balancing the demands of conducting thorough research and writing up findings in a clear and concise manner required careful planning and time management.
  • Incorporating Feedback: Integrating feedback from formative assessments and peers effectively to improve the quality of our writing was challenging but crucial for development.

  • Project life cycles and methodologies
  • Impact of risk and uncertainty on projects
  • Developing a risk management plan
  • Managing project change effectively

Activities

  • We explored the concepts of project management, including defining and explaining its principles and practices.

  • We examined how projects can be affected by risk and uncertainty, and how to identify and manage these factors effectively.

Learning Outcomes

  • Ability to define and explain project management concepts.
  • Appreciation for project life cycles and methodologies.
  • Understanding of the technologies and software needed to support remote collaboration in projects.
  • Knowledge on how to assess and manage risks and uncertainties in projects.
  • Skills in preparing risk management plans and controlling project changes effectively.

Challenges and Difficulties

  • Understanding and applying the different project management methodologies to specific projects.
  • Assessing and planning for risks that are not immediately visible at the start of a project.
  • Managing and implementing changes during a project while maintaining control over scope, cost, and time.
  • Developing comprehensive risk management plans that cover all potential risks and uncertainties.
  • Utilizing the appropriate technologies and software for effective remote project management and collaboration.
