Unit 1

Introduction to Machine Learning (ML)

Activities

During this unit, we undertook the following activities:
  • Studied the historical context of machine learning, its evolution, and current advancements.
  • Explored the opportunities and challenges presented by algorithms in machine learning.
  • Investigated the connection between big data analytics, machine learning, and artificial intelligence.
  • Engaged in group discussions, practical exercises, and critical reading to grasp ML concepts.

Learning Outcomes

  • Developed a deeper understanding of the role of machine learning in the evolution of industry.
  • Identified the key skill sets required for proficiency in machine learning.
  • Learned about potential pitfalls of machine learning and ways to address them.

Challenges and Difficulties

  • Grasping the complex mathematical foundation of certain ML algorithms was challenging initially.
  • The concept of bias-variance tradeoff required a good amount of effort to comprehend.
  • The ethical considerations of applying machine learning in real-world situations were intricate to understand and balance.

Reflection

  • Machine learning, impacting everything from online habits to major financial decisions, has a significant role in daily life.
  • The readings provided a strong foundation in understanding machine learning's core concepts and applications, further strengthened by active discussions and exercises.
  • While the introduction to machine learning posed certain challenges, the foundation built will be beneficial for future units.
  • The insight into potential challenges and pitfalls has equipped us with a more critical and informed perspective for future learning.
  • Looking forward to learning more about the transformative potential of machine learning in upcoming units.

Unit 2

Exploratory Data Analysis

Activities

  • Understanding the steps involved with Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA).
  • Getting familiar with understanding a dataset through feature exploration.
  • Learning how to spot anomalies within the dataset.
  • Learning visual analysis of the dataset.

Learning Outcomes

  • Ability to undertake basic EDA.
  • Better understanding of the dataset.
  • Proficiency in preparing the dataset for machine learning.

Challenges and Difficulties

  • Understanding the Concept: Fully grasping the concept of Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) was initially challenging due to its wide array of techniques and approaches. By going through the readings multiple times and engaging in additional online tutorials, I was able to develop a solid understanding of EDA.
  • Dealing with Unclean Data: The datasets I worked with occasionally contained missing values, inconsistent entries, and outliers, posing a challenge during the practical application of EDA. Through the application of various data cleaning techniques such as filling missing values, removing duplicates, and handling outliers, I was able to improve the quality of my data analysis.
  • Visual Analysis: I initially struggled with applying visual analysis for EDA effectively. Understanding how to use various graphs and charts to explore and understand data was complex. However, by choosing the right visual representation depending on the nature of the data and the insights I aimed to extract, I was able to improve this skill over time.

Additional Reading

  • Miroslav, K. (2021) An Introduction to Machine Learning. 3rd Ed. Springer. Chapter 1.4.
  • Patil, P. (2018) What is Exploratory Data Analysis?
  • Harmadi, A. (2021) 10 Things to do when conducting your Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA).
  • Mostak, T. (2021) How Visualization is Transforming Exploratory Data Analysis.

Reflections

  • Understanding EDA: Exploratory Data Analysis was initially challenging. But after revisiting the study materials and supplementing it with additional online tutorials, I got a solid grip on the EDA concepts.
  • Handling Data: The hands-on experience of dealing with unclean data provided me with practical insights. I learned various data cleaning techniques, enhancing my problem-solving skills in real-world scenarios.
  • Visual Analysis: Initially, I struggled with applying visual analysis effectively. With practice, I got better at choosing the right visual representations, which in turn improved my understanding of data.

Collaborative Discussion 1: The 4th Industrial Revolution (Summary Post)

The progression into Industry 4.0 has underscored the impacts of automation and digitalization on the nature of work and the labour market. As indicated in my initial post, these shifts hold the potential to enhance job quality, productivity, and innovation but also pose challenges like job displacement and the digital skills gap (Schwab, 2016; Arntz, Gregory, & Zierahn, 2016; Bessen, 2019; World Economic Forum, 2020).

The discussion with my peer, Astrid, emphasized the practical complications associated with automation, particularly around self-checkout systems in retail. Her perspective highlighted potential drawbacks such as technical errors, accessibility issues, and susceptibility to theft, which can cause frustration among customers (Chan, 2022; Raydiant, 2021).

In response to these concerns, I mentioned a study that associates self-checkout systems with an increased risk of theft, and an article that illustrates the challenges faced by disabled individuals with these systems, including difficulties navigating touchscreens and physical inaccessibility (Taylor, 2016; Deighton, 2022).

This exchange reinforced my understanding of the nuanced impacts of Industry 4.0 and the importance of not only addressing these challenges through strategies such as continuous learning, training, and policy support but also ensuring that technological advancements are inclusive and user-friendly. Therefore, balancing efficiency with inclusivity and user-friendliness should be at the forefront when planning automation and digitalization strategies.

References:
  • Schwab, K. (2016). The Fourth Industrial Revolution: what it means, how to respond. World Economic Forum.
  • Arntz, M., Gregory, T., & Zierahn, U. (2016). The Risk of Automation for Jobs in OECD Countries: A Comparative Analysis. OECD Social, Employment and Migration Working Papers, No. 189, OECD Publishing, Paris.
  • Bessen, J. E. (2019). AI and Jobs: The Role of Demand. NBER Working Paper No. 24235.
  • World Economic Forum. (2020). The Future of Jobs Report 2020. World Economic Forum, Geneva.
  • Taylor, E. (2016). Supermarket self-checkouts and retail theft: The curious case of the SWIPERS. Criminology & Criminal Justice, 16(5), 552-567.
  • Raydiant. (2021). The State of Self-Service Checkouts. Available from: https://www.raydiant.com/blog/the-state-of-self-service-checkouts [Accessed 22 May 2023].
  • Deighton, K. (2022). Disabled Shoppers Struggle With Inaccessible Self-Checkouts. The Wall Street Journal. Available from: https://www.wsj.com/articles/disabled-shoppers-struggle-with-inaccessible-self-checkouts-11668808226 [Accessed 22 May 2023].
  • Chan, W. (2022). ‘Unexpected item’: how self-checkouts failed to live up to their promise. The Guardian. Available from: https://www.theguardian.com/business/2022/dec/16/self-checkout-us-retail-walmart [Accessed 22 May 2023].

Reflections

  • Correlation and Regression Concepts: Initially, I found it challenging to understand the mathematics behind these statistical techniques. However, through consistent learning and application, I developed a deeper understanding of these concepts.
  • Practical Application: Experimenting with various programs in Codio/Jupyter Notebook gave me first-hand experience in applying these techniques and understanding their real-world implications.
  • Overcoming Challenges: Dealing with multi-collinearity in multiple linear regression was difficult at first. But with time, I learned how to identify and address this issue, which further honed my problem-solving skills.

Unit 4

Exploratory Data Analysis

Activities

  • Studied how to apply the fundamentals of linear regression in Scikit-Learn.
  • Learned to model linear relationships between a single independent and dependent variable.
  • Developed skills in creating a multivariate linear regression model.
  • Evaluated the effectiveness of various models.

Learning Outcomes

  • Able to undertake regression modelling with complex datasets.
  • Able to evaluate results to optimize the model.
  • Expanded knowledge about the scikit-learn library in Python.

Challenges and Difficulties

  • Understanding the complexity of linear relationships in regression models.
  • Optimizing model performance based on evaluation results.
  • Applying scikit-learn library for correlation regression analysis.

Additional Reading

  • Miroslav, K. (2021) An Introduction to Machine Learning. 3rd Ed. Springer. Chapter 4.5.
  • scikit-learn (n.d) Supervised learning.
  • Brownlee, J. (2018) How to Calculate Correlation Between Variables in Python.

Reflection

  • Applying linear regression in Scikit-Learn: I found this to be a practical tool for modeling linear relationships, and it provided a hands-on approach to understanding regression concepts.
  • Developing and evaluating models: This was an invaluable skill that not only allowed me to create effective models but also gave me insights into model optimization.
  • Using scikit-learn library: Initially, using the scikit-learn library was a challenge due to its extensive functionality. However, as I practiced more, I found it to be a versatile tool for machine learning tasks.
